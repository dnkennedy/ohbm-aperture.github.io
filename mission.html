Our Mission.

The purpose of Aperture is to enable a diverse approach to sharing and communicating high quality, community-based, open neuroscience while bringing transparency and interactivity to the publishing process.

About Aperture.

First conceived in 2017 by the Organization for Human Brain Mapping (OHBM), Aperture was created by and for members of OHBM to share and promote their research beyond the traditional PDF.

The scope of Aperture publications includes traditionally published material such as original research reports, reviews, tutorials, and educational materials, but also extends to computational notebooks, interactive plots, software, data, and post-publication peer-review to create reusable, and reproducible Research Objects.

Aperture features peer-reviewed works in all areas of brain imaging and assessment, including those at the interface of other disciplines, with the goal of elevating research standards and accelerating the dissemination of useful tools and information.

The platform will operate as an open-access journal with a double-open Peer Review system. However, Peer Reviewers will have the option to opt-out of having their identity known to the author at any stage in the review process. Research will be published under a Creative Commons Attribution License. There are no submission fees, but authors will be asked to pay an Author Processing Charge upon acceptance for publication.
